Top 10 historical palces in INDIA

"Where history, culture, and colors merge welcome to India."

TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 for being “the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

Agra Fort

Agra Fort is a historical fort in the city of Agra in India. It was the main residence of the emperors of the Mughal Dynasty till 1638, when the capital was shifted from Agra to Delhi. The Agra fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Red Fort

The Red Fort is one of the most significant monuments in the country. India's Prime Minister hoists the National Flag from the ramparts of the Red Fort each year on Independence Day.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal is dedicated to Lord Krishna. More than a palace, the Hawa Mahal is also a cultural and architectural marvel that reflects a truly harmonious amalgamation of the Hindu Rajput and Islamic Mughal architectural styles.

Konark_Sun_Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Konark Sun Temple is famous for its unique architecture. Its geometrical patterns and carved wheels used to serve as sun dials.

Victoria_Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building on the Maidan in Central Kolkata, having its entrance on the Queen's Way. It was built between 1906 and 1921 by the British government.

Kailasa_Temple,_Ellora

A megalith carved from a rock cliff face, considered as one of the most remarkable cave temples in the world because of its size, architecture and sculptural treatment, and "the climax of the rock-cut phase of Indian architecture".

Khajuraho

Khajuraho has the country's largest group of medieval Hindu and Jain temples, it has been listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986 and is considered one of the "seven wonders" of India.

India Gate

At the centre of New Delhi stands the 42 m high India Gate, an “Arc-de-Triomphe” like archway in the middle of a crossroad.It commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during World War.

Sanchi Stupa

Sanchi Stupa in central India are among the oldest and most mature examples of aniconic arts and free-standing architecture that comprehensively document the history of Buddhism from the 3rd century BCE to the 12th century CE.
